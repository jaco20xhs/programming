French Press

A French press, also known as a cafetière, cafetière à piston, Cafeteria, press pot, coffee press, or coffee plunger, is a coffee brewing device invented by Paolini Ugo and patented by Italian designer Attilio Calimani and Giulio Moneta in 1929.

How to Brew


The french press has a built in filter to keep coffeee grinds out of your cup. Measure the amount of water you will be using then use the following:


  • A french presss
  • A spoon (preferably wooden if using a french press made of glass)
  • Course to medium ground coffee
  • A pot to boil water

  1. Place your pot of water onto the heat source and bring to a boil.
  2. Take the pot off heat and allow to cool for 1-2 minutess.
  3. Place coffee groundss into the french press.
  4. Pour half of the water into the french press and stir for 30 seconds. (Warning: use a wooden spoon to stir if using a glass press.)
  5. Pour in the rest of the water and cover with the top.
  6. Wait 3-5 minutes; the longer you wait, the stronger your coffee.
  7. Slowly press down on the plunger until it reaches the bottom of the presss.
  8. Pour into a cup and enjoy a full bodied cup of coffee.
