Cowboy Coffee

What do the words “cowboy coffee” bring to mind? A campfire? A blue and white-speckled kettle? A rough, sludgy cup o’ joe? Whatever you think about cowboy coffee, if you’re in the specialty-coffee world, it probably isn’t positive. Yet cowboy coffee nonetheless has a lot of devotees.

How to Brew


Cowboy coffee is one of the simplest ways to make your coffee. As long as you have a heat source available, you can brew it anywhere. In addition to water, you will need:


  • A pot
  • A source of heat (such as a stove or a campfire)
  • Coarsely ground coffee
    1. Place your pot of water onto the heat source and let the water warm up.
    2. Add the coffee grinds to the water.
    3. Wait for the water to come to a rolling boil.
    4. Reduce the heat to medium. (For a campfire, take the pot off the fire and place it near the edge.)
    5. Let it boil for 2-3 minutes.
    6. Take it off the heat and let it stand for 1 minute.
    7. (optional) Take a cup of cold water and pour it into the pot. This sinks the grinds to the bottom. (If you have a smaller pot, add less water.)
    8. Pour into a cup and enjoy your coffee!
