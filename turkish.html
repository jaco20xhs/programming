Turkish Coffee

Turkish coffee is a method of preparing very finely ground unfiltered coffee. The same method is used in many Middle Eastern and Southeastern European countries.

Read more on Wikipedia

How to Brew


This brewing method is difficult to master. It takes practice to make a good cup of coffee, but once mastered, the result is worth it. To get started on this challenging brewing method, you will need:


  • A turkish coffee pot
  • A spoon
  • Very finely ground coffee
  • (optional) 1 teaspoon of sugar

  1. Place water, coffee, and sugar into the turkish pot. Stir until all the coffee is saturated.
  2. Place the pot onto the stove on low heat.
  3. After 5 minutes, the coffee will foam and begin to rise. Remove from heat.
  4. Pour the foam into a cup. Place back on heat.
  5. The coffee will rise again. Remove from heat and pour out the foam.
  6. Place back on heat. When the coffee rises again, remove from heat.
  7. Slowly pour out the coffee into the cup.
  8. Enjoy the fruits of your labor.

Tips:


  • The coffee should rise within 5 minutes. If it takes longer, increase the heat.
  • Always keep an eye on the coffee. It can easily boil over.
  • Don't let the coffee come to a boil as it will destroy the foam.
  • Tradition dictates to let the coffee rise three times, but many are satisfied with just 1 rise. Experiment and find out what's best for your tastes.
