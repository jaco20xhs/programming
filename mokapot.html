Moka Pot

The moka pot is a stove-top or electric coffee maker that brews coffee by passing boiling water pressurized by steam through ground coffee. Named after the Yemenite city of Mocha, it was invented by an Italian engineer named Alfonso Bialetti in 1933.

How to Brew


For those who want a shot of expresso but can't afford to purchase the expensive equipment necessary to make it. The moka pot is an affordable alternative. This special pot comes in different sizes for different amount of servings. Once you have the right size, you will need the appropriate amount of water and:


  • A moka pot
  • Finely ground coffee
  • (Optional) A separate pot to boil water

  1. Place your pot of water onto the stove. (You can use the bottom part of the moka pot or a separate pot.)
  2. Add the coffee grinds to the moka pot's internal basket.
  3. Place the basket (and water if boiled separately) into the bottom part.
  4. Tightly screw on the top part.
  5. Place the assembled pot onto the stove on medium heat.
  6. Open the top lid and wait 5 minutess. Coffee will eventually rise to the top chamber.
  7. Remove from heat and pour into a cup. You just prepared a shot of espresso from the comfort of your own home.
